Before We Get Into Venues

Two things to know upfront.

December weather. Okinawa in mid-December averages 19-21°C (66-70°F) during the day, dropping to 16-17°C at night. The mildest winter in Japan. No typhoon risk, no rainy season. Wind picks up along the coast and overcast days are possible, but it is comfortable for an outdoor or semi-outdoor ceremony. On the outer islands (Ishigaki, Miyako), December runs 1-2°C warmer with slightly more sunshine.

Chapel capacity. Almost every resort chapel in Okinawa seats 40-60 people. For 70 guests, that means either a small number watch from an adjacent area, or the ceremony moves to a ballroom or terrace. One exception exists (we will get to it). This is standard for destination weddings in Japan and not a dealbreaker, just something to plan around.

Cultural Elements

The Mongolian Traditions

The fire ceremony and Mongolian outfits are important to you both. We want them woven into the day naturally.

The ceremony begins Western-style. Gantuya walks down the aisle in white. Vows, rings, the moment everyone is here for. Then, before the reception, you both change into Mongolian dress. The fire ceremony happens as a transition: on a terrace, in a garden, or at a dedicated moment during the reception. It becomes the bridge between the two halves of your day.

We will work with the venue to confirm fire is permitted and the space is appropriate. For the resort options, outdoor terraces and garden areas work well. Logistics get confirmed once a venue is selected.
